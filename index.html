PRESS RELEASE - JANUARY 1996

"THE INFERNO CELEBRATES SEVEN YEARS AS VEGAS' HOTTEST DESTINATION!"

LAS VEGAS, NV - The infamous INFERNO Casino and Resort continues to blaze a trail through Las Vegas entertainment, celebrating its seventh anniversary with record attendance and an expanded gaming floor.

"We've doubled down on what makes us unique," says owner Richard "The Devil" Dante. "Our signature 'Hell's Kitchen' restaurant now features celebrity chef demonstrations, and our 'Eternal Flame' lounge hosts the most sizzling acts on the strip."

The casino's iconic 40-foot neon sign, featuring animated flames and a pitchfork-wielding devil, remains the most photographed landmark on the northern strip.

THE MYSTERIOUS FIRE OF '97

On March 15, 1997, a mysterious fire broke out in the main casino floor of THE INFERNO. Despite the casino's flame-themed decor, investigators determined the fire suppression systems had been tampered with. The blaze consumed most of the building within hours.

Miraculously, no casualties were reported, though rumors persist of supernatural warnings that cleared the casino floor minutes before the first flames appeared.

The iconic neon sign survived intact and was donated to the Neon Museum of Las Vegas, though strange electrical phenomena prevented it from ever being displayed publicly.

Owner Richard Dante disappeared the night of the fire. Insurance investigators noted his office safe was found empty and open. His whereabouts remain unknown to this day.
